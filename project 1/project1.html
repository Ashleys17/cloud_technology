GEORGETOWN GUYANA

Georgetown is Guyana’s capital, on South America’s North Atlantic coast. English is there official language, which is the only South American country with English as the official language. Guyana is known for exporting sugar, rice, and tropical fruits, as well as timber, balata, bauxite, gold, and diamonds from the Guyanese hinterland. This is the flag of Guyana also known as the golden arrowhead, this has been the national flag of Guyana since May 1966, when the country became independent from the United Kingdom. It was designed by Whitney Smith. The five colours have symbolic attributions:The green stands for the jungles and fields that cover most of the country, Red represents zeal and sacrifice which are part of the nation-building process that the Guyanese are striving towards, Black indicates the perseverance needed to reach their goal, White represents the country's extensive water resources, the yellow represents Guyana's mineral wealth

The Famous Lighthouse

The Georgetown Lighthouse was first built by the Dutch in 1817 and then rebuilt in 1830 to help guide ships into the Demerara River from the Atlantic Ocean. The 31m (103 feet) high octagonal structure is a famous Georgetown, Guyana landmark with its distinct vertical red and white stripes. The Lighthouse, is maintained by the National Trust of Guyana. The brick structure was commissioned on 1st June 1830, when it replaced a wooden lighthouse that had been built on the same site by the Dutch.

Kaieteur Falls

Kaieteur Falls is known as one of the largest single drop water fall, located on the Potaro river. It sits in the section of the Amazon rainforest included in the Potaro-siparuni region of Guyana. It is 226 metres (741 ft) high, While many falls have greater height, few have the combination of height and water volume, and Kaieteur is among the most powerful waterfalls in the world with an average flow rate of 663 cubic metres per second (23,400 cubic feet per second). Kaieteur Falls is a major tourist attraction in Guyana

The culture

Guyana is made up of six ethnic groups, Indigenous, East Indian, African, Portuguese, European and Chinese. Despite being in South America, Guyana is considered a Caribbean nation. Celebrations in Guyana reflect the diverse origins of its people,typical European holidays such as Easter and Christmas, Diwali, and Holi(Phagwah) from Guyanese Hindus, and Mashramani, a holiday to celebrate Guyana's independence inspired by Amerindian festivals. The are 5 top guyanese festivals, First Mashramani this celebrates Guyana’s becoming of a republic. Thousands of people line the streets to view a big parade with extravagant floats, people dressed in colorful costumes dancing to Caribbean-originated music. Secondly Divali, this known as the The festival of lights, held annually in Guyana during October or November. It is celebrated by Hindus who place clay cups that are lit, around their house. Thirdly Paghwa also called Holi which is known as the festival of colours which takes place annually in March. Those who participate will typically wear white clothing then spray water and throw colored powder at each other. Holi is originally a Hindu celebration which was brought over to Guyana from India in the early 1800s. Next there's Easter a widely celebrated holiday in Guyana. During this time Schools are closed for two weeks and children work with their parents and friends to prepare for a unique Guyanese Easter tradition; kite-flying. When you look up to the skies during Easter, you will see a plethora of creative and colourful kites, since Easter is a Christian celebration, masses are also held at churches. Lastly Independence Day this was where Guyana was ruled by the British until May 26th, 1966, when the country became independent. To celebrate this momentous day, Independence Day festivities are held annually including a flag-raising ceremony, fireworks and a message from the president.

The food

Guyana has a lot of popular dishes and tasty dishes. First Pepper pot, this dish is a meat(your preference) stew with the main ingredient cassareep(a black sauce prepared using cassave roots), this dish is typically eaten with bread and usually served during christmas time. Another dish is cook-up rice this is a traditional one-pot dish that combines rice cooked in coconut milk with peas, meats and beans, Cook-up rice is an essential dish prepared on Old Year’s Night (New Year’s Eve). Next Chicken curry this is a popular Caribbean comfort food prepared using chicken in a thick, savoury curry sauce, this can either be served with roti or rice.

Tourist Attraction

The st.George's cathedral is known as a main tourist attraction it's one of the most impressive building and also one of the largest wooden churches. Apart from its notable wooden construction, St. George’s Cathedral is an important and much-loved landmark in the Guyanese capital of Georgetown for two reasons: most locals would agree that it’s the most impressive building in the capital, and anyone with an eye for history can see how the cathedral is a window into Guyana’s colonial past. The cathedral was completed and consecrated on November 8, 1894.
