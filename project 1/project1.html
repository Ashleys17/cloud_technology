GEORGETOWN GUYANA

Georgetown is Guyana’s capital, on South America’s North Atlantic coast. English is there official language, which is the only South American country with English as the official language. Guyana is known for exporting sugar, rice, and tropical fruits, as well as timber, balata, bauxite, gold, and diamonds from the Guyanese hinterland. This is the flag of Guyana also known as the golden arrowhead, this has been the national flag of Guyana since May 1966, when the country became independent from the United Kingdom. It was designed by Whitney Smith. The five colours have symbolic attributions:The green stands for the jungles and fields that cover most of the country, Red represents zeal and sacrifice which are part of the nation-building process that the Guyanese are striving towards, Black indicates the perseverance needed to reach their goal, White represents the country's extensive water resources, the yellow represents Guyana's mineral wealth

The Famous Lighthouse

The Georgetown Lighthouse was first built by the Dutch in 1817 and then rebuilt in 1830 to help guide ships into the Demerara River from the Atlantic Ocean. The 31m (103 feet) high octagonal structure is a famous Georgetown, Guyana landmark with its distinct vertical red and white stripes. The Lighthouse, is maintained by the National Trust of Guyana. The brick structure was commissioned on 1st June 1830, when it replaced a wooden lighthouse that had been built on the same site by the Dutch.

Kaieteur Falls

Kaieteur Falls is known as one of the largest single drop water fall, located on the Potaro river. It sits in the section of the Amazon rainforest included in the Potaro-siparuni region of Guyana. It is 226 metres (741 ft) high, While many falls have greater height, few have the combination of height and water volume, and Kaieteur is among the most powerful waterfalls in the world with an average flow rate of 663 cubic metres per second (23,400 cubic feet per second). Kaieteur Falls is a major tourist attraction in Guyana
